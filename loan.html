Loans

What Is a Loan?

A loan is the cash value plus dividends, less any unpaid loan and accrued interest. Policy owners can borrow using the policy as sole security.

How a Loan Works

Loans can be processed if the policy contains a loan provision and there is sufficient value for the amount requested. Loan value is a percentage of a policyâ€™s guaranteed cash value plus dividend value. A loan can be obtained from New York Life (NYL) or New York Life Insurance and Annuity Corporation (NYLIAC), using the policy as sole security, and does not have to be repaid, however loan interest is due annually. The specific criteria for processing loans are outlined in this procedure. A policy owner may request a cash loan for a specific amount, the maximum amount, a loan exclusive of dividends or a loan to pay premiums on the same or another policy or application, according to their personal preference or needs.


Key Points

  • Excess loans increase everyday until loan is paid off.
  • Loan on a policy will affect Net Cash Value and the face amount.
  • Loan interest will be effected if policy gets put on Check "O" Matic.
  • Interest not paid when due becomes part of the loan and will also bear interest.
  • Whole life policies, loan interest is brought up to the loan transaction date
  • Target life policies, loan interest is paid to the next anniversary

Points to Remember

  • No loan value on term policies OR extended term insurance
  • Loan requests on Variable Life Insurance (VLI) policies should be referred to VPSC for handling.
  • Max loan amount that can be processed automatically is $200,000.00
